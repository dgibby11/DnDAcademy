Forest begins at the southwest perimeter; used for wilderness training

Positioned in the southwest for direct wilderness access. The adjacent forest is a known training ground. Rangers faculty are also responsible for maintaining the owlbear containment facility and managing creature specimens on campus grounds.

The forest adjacent to the department extends far enough that a loose creature (such as an owlbear) could conceal itself within it for 2–3 days before rangers locate it. This has occurred at least once in documented campus history.
