Mormesk the Wraith

CREATURE · Named Creatures

The spirit of the last wizard to die in the catastrophic battle that sealed [[wave_echo_cave]] five centuries ago. Mormesk was a powerful human mage working alongside the dwarf and gnome miners; when the explosion consumed the cave, his body was destroyed but his spirit refused to depart — bound by the unfinished work of his life and the trauma of his death.

He haunts the northeast section of Wave Echo Cave, particularly his old workroom and the area near the wooden chest that holds his remaining possessions.

Tactics

  • CR 5 — incorporeal, resistant to most damage types, immune to poison and necrotic.
  • Life Drain: +6 to hit, 3d6+4 necrotic damage (DC 14 Con save or max HP reduced).
  • Incorporeal Movement: moves through creatures and objects.
  • Create Specter: humanoids slain by Mormesk may rise as specters.

DM Notes

Mormesk can be negotiated with — he's intelligent and was once a person of principle. He despises the undead that now infest his cave (they are not his doing — they rose from the battle dead independently). He loathes the Black Spider for desecrating the mine.

If approached respectfully — and especially if the party offers to honor his memory, clear the undead from his cave, or deal with Nezznar — Mormesk may stand aside and allow passage. He might even provide information about the cave's layout. What he wants is acknowledgment: he died protecting this place. The least anyone can do is remember that.

His wooden chest contains: 1,100 cp, 160 sp, a bone pipe carved with arcane symbols (worth 25 gp), a spell scroll of Augury, and a spell scroll of Charm Person.

[[hamun_kost]] at [[old_owl_well]] wants information about Mormesk's death — specifically what spells he was casting when he died. This is actually something Mormesk can tell the party directly.
