Spectator

CREATURE · Named Creatures

A beholder-kin — a four-eyed floating sphere summoned five centuries ago to guard the [[forge_of_spells|Forge of Spells]] in [[wave_echo_cave]]. The spectator has been faithfully at its post ever since, even though its summoner died in the battle that destroyed the mine. It has lost track of time in the darkness, and its grip on reality has grown tenuous.

Tactics

  • CR 3 — formidable at lower levels due to its eye rays and immunity to several conditions.
  • Bite: +3 to hit, 1d8+1 piercing.
  • Eye Rays (3/turn): Confusion Ray (DC 13 Wis or confused), Paralyzing Ray (DC 13 Con or paralyzed), Fear Ray (DC 13 Wis or frightened), Wounding Ray (+5 to hit, 3d10 necrotic).
  • Spell Reflection: if a spell fails to affect the spectator, it reflects back to the caster.

DM Notes

The spectator can be negotiated with. It's confused about how long it's been — the constant darkness and solitude have blurred its sense of time. A successful DC 15 Deception check can convince it that the party are the "authorized agents" of the original summoner. Alternatively, pointing out that its contract term has obviously expired (500 years is more than any reasonable term) requires a DC 15 Persuasion check but works.

If the party negotiates successfully, the spectator departs peacefully. It's not hostile for the sake of it — it genuinely just wants to complete its job, and "job complete, contract expired" is a satisfying resolution for it.

After any resolution (fight or negotiation), the [[forge_of_spells]] is accessible. The [[lightbringer]] and [[dragonguard]] are on stands near the forge.
