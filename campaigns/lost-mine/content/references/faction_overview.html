Five Factions — Quick Reference

REFERENCE · Campaign

Five major factions are represented by NPCs in Phandalin. Each can be joined by party members who complete faction quests. Each offers different benefits for future play.

Faction Local Rep Quest Ethos
[[lords_alliance|Lords' Alliance]] [[sildar_hallwinter]] Find Glasstaff / restore order Civilization, stability, lawful authority
[[harpers|The Harpers]] [[sister_garaele]] Visit [[agatha]] about [[bowgentles_spellbook]] Freedom, equality, covert good
[[order_of_the_gauntlet|Order of the Gauntlet]] [[daran_edermath]] Investigate [[old_owl_well]] Devout, militant, proactive against evil
[[emerald_enclave|Emerald Enclave]] [[reidoth]] Deal with [[venomfang]] at Thundertree Nature, balance, wilderness protection
[[zhentarim|The Zhentarim]] [[halia_thornton]] Get Glasstaff's letters, don't ask questions Power, wealth, pragmatism, grey morality

DM Notes — Running the Factions

Don't push faction membership on the party — let it emerge from their choices. If they help Garaele and seem aligned with the Harpers' values, she mentions it naturally. A party that clears out Redbrands without asking for anything might attract Sildar's Lords' Alliance attention unbidden.

Faction membership is a long-term hook, not a mechanical requirement for finishing the adventure. Players who don't join any faction miss some rewards and future hooks, but they can still complete Lost Mine of Phandelver without issues.
