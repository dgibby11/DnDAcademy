Lost Mine of Phandelver — Overview

REFERENCE · Campaign

Background

More than five hundred years ago, a human mage and the dwarf Urmon made an agreement known as the Phandelver Pact. The dwarves and gnomes of the Phandelver region shared knowledge of their ancient mine — Wave Echo Cave — in exchange for the human mages' help in channeling the cave's magical properties into the Forge of Spells. Together they enchanted weapons and armor, and the operation thrived for generations.

Orc armies overran the area and destroyed the settlement. In the battle for Wave Echo Cave, the mages and fighters inside triggered a magical explosion that sealed the mine. The location was lost. The three families who knew it — including the Rockseekers — passed down the secret, searching for generations.

The Present

[[gundren_rockseeker]] has found it. He and his brothers located the cave using old maps and family records. Gundren hired the party to escort a supply wagon to Phandalin while he rode ahead with [[sildar_hallwinter]]. He never arrived — because a drow called [[nezznar_black_spider|the Black Spider]] had been watching, and moved first.

The Structure

  • Part 1 — Goblin Arrows: The road to Phandalin. The goblin ambush, [[cragmaw_hideout]], rescuing [[sildar_hallwinter]].
  • Part 2 — Phandalin: The town. The Redbrand problem. The [[redbrand_hideout]]. The NPC web of quests and factions.
  • Part 3 — The Spider's Web: The wilderness. [[ruins_of_thundertree]], [[cragmaw_castle]], optional sites. Finding the way to the cave.
  • Part 4 — Wave Echo Cave: The lost mine. The undead. The Black Spider. The Forge of Spells. The end.

The Five Factions

Each major NPC in Phandalin represents one of the five major factions of the Forgotten Realms. See [[faction_overview]] for a quick reference. Party members may join one or more factions during the course of the adventure.
