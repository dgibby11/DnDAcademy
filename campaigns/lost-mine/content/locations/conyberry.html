Conyberry & Agatha's Lair

LOCATION · Wilderness

Conyberry is an abandoned village — crumbled buildings, empty wells, not a soul living. No one has lived here in years; the place feels oppressively silent. A trail leads into the forest to the west, ending at a decrepit stone cottage: the lair of [[agatha]], a banshee.

Agatha's Lair

The interior of the cottage is kept immaculate by supernatural force — dustless shelves, neatly arranged objects, candles burning with cold blue flame. Agatha herself appears as a translucent elven woman of terrible beauty, her face hollow with centuries of grief and rage.

DM Notes

[[agatha]] is approached by the party on behalf of [[sister_garaele]] (see [[bowgentles_spellbook]]). The party must present Agatha with a gift — a jeweled silver comb — and then ask their question politely.

Agatha's answer: She traded Bowgentle's spellbook to a necromancer named Tsernoth, from the city of Iriaebor, more than a century ago. The book is long gone; she doesn't know where it is now. But she honors the question if the gift is presented correctly — she's vain, and a sincere gesture of respect calms her considerably.

DC: If the party is rude, she attacks. If they approach respectfully (Charisma check DC 15, or simply roleplay it well), she answers one question truthfully. The comb is not a bribe — it's an act of respect; give advantage on the check if they present it first without asking for the favor.

Agatha is a banshee (CR 4). Don't let combat start unless you want a very hard fight — her Horrifying Visage can paralyze the whole party. Roleplay is strongly the intended path here.
