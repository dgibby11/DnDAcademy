Redbrand Hideout

LOCATION · Dungeons

A cellar complex beneath [[tresendar_manor]], carved partly from stone and partly from natural rock. The [[redbrands]] use it as their base of operations under the direction of [[iarno_albrek|Glasstaff]].

Key Areas

  • Crevasse (Area 1): A natural fissure splits the cellar. A rickety wooden bridge crosses it. The [[nothic]] lairs in the crevasse — a mutated wizard driven mad by forbidden knowledge. Glasstaff feeds it scraps and it stays loyal.
  • Guard Barracks (Areas 2, 3): Two groups of Redbrand ruffians. Usually drinking or sleeping.
  • Crypt (Area 4): Three old sarcophagi. Skeletons stir if the sarcophagi are disturbed.
  • Slave Pens (Area 5): A locked door hides cells containing the Dendrar family (Mirna, her daughter, and her son) — kidnapped when Thel Dendrar stood up to the Redbrands.
  • Armory (Area 6): Weapons cache; includes [[talon]] in a barrel of sand.
  • Bugbear Quarters (Area 7): Two bugbears and their wolf serve as Glasstaff's enforcers.
  • Glasstaff's Quarters (Area 8): Wizard's lab and bedroom. The [[staff_of_defense]] leans against the desk. On the desk: a letter from the Black Spider revealing Glasstaff's identity and mission.

DM Notes

The letter in Glasstaff's quarters is a critical clue — it names Iarno Albrek, confirms he answers to the [[black_spider_identity|Black Spider]], and orders him to drive strangers out of Phandalin and capture the Rockseeker brothers if possible. Show this to the party — it's a major revelation and advances both [[glasstaff_identity]] and [[black_spider_identity]].

[[iarno_albrek]] flees if warned — he has a back exit via a tunnel to the south of the manor. If he escapes, he can become a recurring antagonist; if captured, [[sildar_hallwinter]] wants him handed over to Neverwinter justice (wants him alive).

The [[nothic]] is willing to barter. It uses Weird Insight to learn secrets and can be negotiated with — it knows about the letter and about [[talon]]. It will stand aside if offered a gift of meat. But it's fundamentally dangerous and unpredictable.

Mirna's Heirloom: Once freed, Mirna Dendrar tells the party about an herb and alchemy shop her family owned in [[ruins_of_thundertree]] before they fled. In a stone box behind the counter: an emerald necklace worth 200 gp. She gives this information freely as thanks (see [[mirnas_heirloom]]).
