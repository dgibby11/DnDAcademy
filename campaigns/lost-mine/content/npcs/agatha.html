Agatha

NPC · Notable Creatures

A banshee haunting the forest near the ruins of [[conyberry]]. In life she was a powerful elven mage; in death she has persisted for centuries, bound to her lair by pride, grief, and the weight of her accumulated knowledge. She keeps her cottage immaculate by supernatural force and receives visitors with cold contempt.

She knows the answer to the party's question about [[bowgentles_spellbook]]: she traded Bowgentle's spellbook to a necromancer named Tsernoth from Iriaebor, over a century ago. The book is gone.

DM Notes

Agatha is CR 4 — a genuinely dangerous fight if it comes to it. Her Horrifying Visage (DC 13 Wis save or frightened for 1 minute) can ruin the party's action economy; her Corrupting Touch deals significant necrotic damage. Don't let combat happen unless the players force it through arrogance.

The roleplay path: Present the silver jeweled comb as a gift before asking anything. A character who does this without prompting — out of genuine respect — should get advantage on the subsequent Charisma check (DC 15). If the players treat her as a monster to be managed, raise the DC accordingly. She responds to sincerity and to beauty.

She will answer exactly one question. She will not answer follow-up questions or bargain for more. She knows she has the information the party wants and enjoys the power dynamic.
